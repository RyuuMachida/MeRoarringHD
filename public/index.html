How to Setup Android via USB

  1. 1
    Enable Developer Options:

    On your phone, go to Settings > About Phone. Tap Build Number 7 times until you see a message saying "You are now a developer!".

  2. 2
    Enable USB Debugging:

    Go to Settings > System > Developer Options. Scroll down and enable USB Debugging.

  3. 3
    Connect phone to Laptop:

    Connect your phone via a USB cable. When a prompt appears on your phone screen asking to "Allow USB Debugging?", check "Always allow from this computer" and tap Allow.

  4. 4
    Click "Scan Devices" & Start:

    Click the Scan Devices button in the header. Select your phone from the dropdown list, then click Start Screen Mirror.

Wireless Screen Mirroring (Android Wi-Fi)

Make sure both your laptop and phone are connected to the exact same Wi-Fi network.

  1. 1
    Initial USB Setup:

    Perform the USB setup once by connecting your phone via cable and authorizing USB Debugging.

  2. 2
    Find your Phone's IP Address:

    On your phone, go to Settings > About Phone > Status (or Wi-Fi settings) to find your local IP Address (e.g., 192.168.1.50).

  3. 3
    Enable TCP Port over ADB:

    Open the console logs here or a Windows terminal, run ADB in TCP mode: adb tcpip 5555. (You can run this by entering it in the command executor or starting mirroring once over USB which initializes ADB).

  4. 4
    Connect Wirelessly:

    Disconnect your USB cable. Open a terminal and run: adb connect <YOUR_PHONE_IP>:5555. Once connected, your phone IP will show up in the "Select Active Device" dropdown wirelessly!

How to Mirror iPhone (iOS) Screen to Windows

Since iOS screen mirroring is locked to Apple's proprietary AirPlay protocol, it cannot be run directly via USB debugging like Android. However, you can make your PC act as an AirPlay Receiver!

  1. 1
    Connect to the same Wi-Fi:

    Ensure your iPhone and Windows laptop are connected to the same local Wi-Fi network.

  2. 2
    Use UxPlay (Open Source AirPlay Server) or 5KPlayer:

    Download and install UxPlay (open-source AirPlay server port for Windows) or a free receiver like 5KPlayer or LonelyScreen.

    Note: For UxPlay, you need to install Apple's Bonjour Print Services for Windows (which enables local network device discovery).

  3. 3
    Activate Screen Mirroring on iPhone:

    Swipe down from the top-right corner of your iPhone to open the Control Center. Tap the Screen Mirroring icon (two overlapping rectangles) and select your laptop from the list. Your screen and audio will stream immediately to your PC!
